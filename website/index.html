Strategies and Features

Integrated solutions for structural stability, thermal comfort, and sustainability.

Local-Low cost materials

Casuarina wood is considered a low-cost building material because it is one of the most common tree species in Egypt. According to FAO data, casuarina plantations cover approximately 44,000 hectares, ensuring high availability, reduced transportation costs, and economic feasibility for construction applications.

Thermal Insulation

Thermal performance is enhanced by combining an external bamboo layer with internal rice-straw insulation. Bamboo provides mechanical protection, while rice straw offers high thermal resistance due to its low conductivity. The composite wall achieves a low U-value that is significantly lower than that of conventional brick walls (‚âà 2 W/m¬≤¬∑K), resulting in reduced heat transfer and improved indoor thermal comfort.

V-bracing support system

The V-bracing support system resists lateral loads to improve building stability. It uses two diagonal members forming a V shape that efficiently converts wind-induced horizontal forces into axial tension and compression in the diagonal members. This increases the overall lateral stiffness of the building. Calculated axial forces and stresses remain well below the allowable limits of Casuarina wood, confirming structural safety.

Cross-Ventilation Dynamics

Cross-ventilation operates based on pressure differences created by wind flow around the building. Proper positioning and sizing of inlet and outlet openings generate continuous airflow through the interior. This airflow removes warm air and replaces it with cooler outdoor air, resulting in a high air-change rate that improves thermal comfort and supports passive cooling.
